Microsoft 365 Enterprise Training

Role: Instructional Designer & Facilitator

Tools: OneDrive SharePoint Teams Copilot AI Power Apps OneNote Loop Excel Outlook Forms

Project Overview

I created and delivered an enterprise-wide Microsoft 365 training. This training was offered to the entire organization and consisted of learners from a variety of roles and departments. A key theme across the organization was that team members had access to a wide range of Microsoft 365 tools, but lacked clarity on when and how to use them effectively. There were also a significant differences in understanding between teammates in corporate versus retail roles.

This project addressed this gap by designing and delivering a targeted, full-day learning experience, open to all employees, which focused on providing hands-on experience, improving confidence, and enabling more efficient day-to-day workflows across customer-facing and support teams. The workshop was intentionally positioned as an entry-point experience — helping learners orient themselves within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem before pursuing deeper, role-specific training.

Planning & Discovery

The planning phase focused on identifying gaps in awareness and usability. Early inputs included feedback gathered from interviews (formal and informal), observing and seeking information on workflows, and formal surveys from across the organization. Information Technology served as a key partner, providing usage data and additional survey results that shaped content, priorities and delivery-related decisions.

Solution Strategy Principles

  • Breadth over depth — reduce uncertainty and increase adoption before pursuing mastery
  • Scenario-based relevance — content tied directly to everyday work activities
  • Scalable delivery — designed for in-person pilot with a clear path to virtual expansion

Feedback Integration

Survey insights and usage data (primarily from IT reports) directly guided topic prioritization, session pacing, and decisions around scaling. This data-informed approach ensured the workshop addressed real organizational needs rather than assumed ones.

Instructional Design Approach

Based on information collected during the planning phase, we decided that an in-person "workshop" format (rather than a virtual or eLearning format) was most appropriate in this case. Surveys and other information indicated that employees interested in this learning opportunity not only wanted hands-on experiences, but also facilitator(s) physically present who could answer questions and offer support in real time. We also wanted to be able to customize activities and responses based on the roles and contexts of the learners in each session. In addition, it was determined that a virtual format would not be ideal for a full-day session (although virtual sessions on specific topics were possible at a later time). Particularly for a full-day session, the topics were carefully planned to offer "bite-sized" and experiential learning opportunities, which encouraged hands-on learning. Moreover, core design principles emphasized practical orientation over deep technical instruction.

Design Principles

  • Tool clarity: Helping learners understand which tool to use and when
  • Ecosystem mindset: Demonstrating how tools work together rather than in isolation
  • Cognitive load management: Avoiding deep dives in favor of practical, everyday uses
  • Clear learning pathways: Providing next steps for continued skill-building

Core Content Areas

  • OneDrive, SharePoint, Teams, and Copilot AI as foundational tools
  • Integration of AI/Copilot into everyday workflows
  • Introductory awareness of Power Apps for process improvement
  • Real-world use cases tied to communication, file management, and collaboration

Learning Flow & Delivery

The workshop was structured as a full-day, progressive learning experience — intentionally sequenced to build understanding while maintaining engagement through varied pacing and interaction. Content was organized into four thematic areas, each reinforcing how Microsoft 365 tools work together rather than in isolation.

[Workshop] Full-day instructor-led session initialized
[Format]   In-person pilot → scalable to virtual
[Status]   Phased rollout with structured feedback collection

📁 File Management & Storage

Best practices for creating, organizing, and accessing content using OneDrive and SharePoint — establishing a shared foundation before introducing collaboration tools. We also addressed the relationship between Microsoft Teams and OneDrive/SharePoint, which the planning phase had indicated was a common point of misunderstanding.

🤝 Communication & Collaboration

Teams, Outlook, and Forms covered as a package to show how communication, feedback, and coordination-related tools complement each other in daily work.

🤖 AI & Automation

Copilot AI and Power Apps introduced as practical productivity accelerators — connecting familiar workflows with emerging capabilities for automation and intelligent assistance. PowerApps were introduced in a brief session that focused on demonstrating a relatively simple "app" learners could re-create. Copilot AI was featured in its own session, in which we discussed the foundations of effective prompting and provided hands-on learning opportunities to teammates, encouraging them to customize the sample prompts we demonstrated to fit their role(s).

📝 Knowledge & Content Management

OneNote, Loop, and Excel explored as tools for capturing, organizing, and analyzing information — rounding out a complete picture of the Microsoft 365 ecosystem.

Key Challenge

The Hurdle: Covering ten distinct tools in a single day risked overwhelming learners and diluting the value of each topic. Without careful design, a broad survey course could have left participants more confused than when they started.

The Fix: The solution was intentional cognitive load management — designed for awareness rather than mastery, using scenario-based content tied to real workflows, and embedding structured breaks and "play" periods throughout the workshop. Framing tools in relation to each other (the ecosystem mindset) gave learners mental hooks to distinguish and remember each one, turning the length of the overall session into an advantage rather than a liability.

Outcomes & Skills

📈 Increased tool adoption & confidence 🔁 Reduced workflow inefficiencies 🤝 Improved cross-team collaboration 🗺️ Clear on-ramp to continued upskilling
  • Needs Analysis: Partnered with IT to leverage usage data and survey feedback to identify verified gaps and prioritize essential content. In addition, a survey was sent to learners before each individual session, helping us customize content and areas of emphasis.
  • Curriculum Design: Built a full-day learning journey with intentional sequencing, pacing, and cognitive load principles applied throughout.
  • Stakeholder Collaboration: Coordinated across IT and business teams to validate demand, manage pilot logistics, and plan for scalable delivery.
  • Facilitation: Delivered instructor-led sessions adaptable to varied learner familiarity levels with real-time interaction and feedback loops. We planned to allow for topics to be expanded or contracted (within established limits) based on interest and engagement.
  • Data-Informed Iteration: Used pilot session feedback and registration data to guide content refinement and decisions around future virtual offerings.

Final Result: The Microsoft 365 Sampler workshop successfully addressed a widespread digital fluency gap, giving teammates the awareness and confidence to navigate the tools already available to them — and a clear path to building deeper skills over time. The phased pilot approach validated demand and set the foundation for broader, scalable delivery.
